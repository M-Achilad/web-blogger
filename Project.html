TOP 10 RICHEST ATHLETES IN THE WORLD

#1. $130 MILLION

LIONEL MESSI

Image Credit-wikipedia

Age: 35 | Sport: Soccer | Nationality: Argentina

The $20 million-per-year deal between Lionel Messi and Socios expands his list of sponsors, which already includes PepsiCo, Budweiser, and Adidas. In a partnership established in June of last year, he also became Hard Rock International's first athlete brand ambassador, enabling Messi to match Cristiano Ronaldo of Manchester United in terms of off-field earnings for the first time since 2013. Messi was named the world's best men's soccer player in 2021 and won the Ballon d'Or in that year, but he has struggled on the field more recently, scoring just nine goals in 32 appearances for Paris Saint-Germain after tallying 38 in 47 games in his final season with Barcelona. However, despite PSG's failure in the Round of 16 of the Champions League, the team won the French Ligue 1 championship in Messi's debut campaign.

#2. $121.2 MILLION

LEBRON JAMES

Image Credit-wikipedia

Age: 37 | Sport: Basketball | Nationality: U.S

Although the Los Angeles Lakers didn't make the playoffs this year, LeBron James has never been more powerful off the field. He appeared in Space Jam: A New Legacy the previous year, and he recently switched the home of his chat program, The Shop, from HBO to YouTube. Forbes estimates that in October, he sold a sizable minority stake in SpringHill, the production firm behind both projects, for around $725 million, bringing his total wealth to $850 million. James starred alongside a computer-generated version of his younger self in a Super Bowl commercial after establishing an endorsement arrangement with Crypto.com in January. Additionally, he most recently made investments in StatusPRO, a sports technology startup that develops virtual reality training solutions, and Tonal, a home gym company.

#3. $115 MILLION

CRISTIANO RONALDO

Image Credit-wikipedia

Age: 37 | Sport: Soccer | Nationality: Portugal

Cristiano Ronaldo, like his adversary Lionel Messi, has struggled in his debut season at Manchester United, who are currently sitting in sixth position in the Premier League standings with one game remaining. There are currently rumors circulating that Ronaldo, who previously played for Manchester United from 2003 to 2009, may move again during the summer transfer window. Ronaldo's extensive social media presence contributes significantly to his income potential: Considering that he has 690 million followers across Twitter, Facebook, and Instagram, he has the power to demand exorbitant fees from sponsors like Nike, Herbalife, and Clear shampoo. Additionally, he invests in Tatel eateries, which now have a Beverly Hills location, and is the face of the upcoming app ZujuGP, a future app that seeks to create a virtual soccer community.

#4. $95 MILLION

NEYMAR

Image Credit-wikipedia

Age: 30 | Sport: Soccer | Nationality: Brazil

Neymar reached the 400-goal mark for his career in November, but like Lionel Messi, he was criticized following Paris Saint-early Germain's withdrawal from the Champions League. Now, he will concentrate on the World Cup in Qatar this autumn, which he has hinted might be his last. In addition to his lucrative off-field endorsement deals with Puma and Red Bull, Neymar: The Perfect Chaos, a new Netflix docuseries, features him. He signed up with the NFTSTAR platform in November and spent over $1 million on two Bored Ape Yacht Club NFTs in a single day in January, so he is also getting involved in the NFT world.

#6. $92.1 MILLION

KEVIN DURANT

Image Credit-wikipedia

Age: 33 | Sport: Basketball | Nationality: U.S.

Only LeBron James ($32 million) among active players has a shoe contract worth more than Brooklyn Nets star Kevin Durant's annual salary of over $28 million. With media company Boardroom and investment firm Thirty Five Ventures, his commercial empire goes far beyond sponsorships. He has lately added relationships with Coinbase, NBA Top Shot, and Weedmaps. He recently invested in digital fitness business Future and NFT platform OpenSea, and he also supports SeatGeek's SPAC merger. Rich Kleiman, a longstanding business associate of Durant's, and they also announced last year that they will start their own SPAC, which is still looking for a buyer.

#7. $90.7 MILLION

ROGER FEDERER

Image Credit-wikipedia

Age: 40 | Sport: Tennis | Nationality: Switzerland

Roger Federer was only able to compete in six tournaments in 2020 and 2021 combined due to injuries, and he has yet to play tennis in 2022. Whatever the case, the former No. 1 player in the world continues to be the best sports spokesman, endorsing companies like Uniqlo and Rolex. In 2019, he also made an investment in the rising Swiss shoe manufacturer On, which raised more than $600 million when it went public in September. Federer told Forbes at the time, after spending 20 days in the lab with the On team creating the company's pro tennis shoe, "We work very closely together on product creation."

#8. $90 MILLION

CANELO ALVAREZ

Image Credit-wikipedia

Age: 31 | Sport: Boxing | Nationality: Mexico

With earnings of at least $40 million from his two pay-per-view victories in May and November of last year, Canelo Alvarez is boxing's top draw. His defeat to light-heavyweight champion Dmitry Bivol on May 7, 2022, happened outside of Forbes' tracking window for this list. Alvarez has a successful business relationship with Hennessy outside of the ring and hopes to open a taco restaurant in California in addition to operating one in his native Mexico. In addition to planning a series of fights in Mexico with Matchroom Boxing and DAZN, Alvarez announced last year that he would be opening a chain of gas stations.

#9. $83.9 MILLION

TOM BRADY

Image Credit-wikipedia

Age: 44 | Sport: Football | Nationality: U.S.

After a great 2021 season at age 43, Tom Brady's retirement this off-season lasted less than six weeks, which was good news for the Tampa Bay Buccaneers. Off the field, he's undoubtedly picking up some new skills. Brady cofounded Religion of Sports, a production company, with Pro Football Hall of Famer Michael Strahan and director Gotham Chopra last year. Religion of Sports recently announced a content deal with Skydance Sports. Brady also cofounded Autograph, an NFT platform, which raised $170 million in a Series B funding round announced in January. The next road trip film 80 for Brady is being produced by his second company, 199 Productions, and he also has a new apparel line with the brilliantly titled BRADY.And when it comes time for Brady to end his career in football, he already has his next lucrative job set up: a commentator position with Fox Sports. That contract, at $375 million over ten years, will pay him more than he has made playing in the NFL's 22 seasons, according to the New York Post.

#10. $80.9 MILLION

GIANNIS ANTETOKOUNMPO

Image Credit-wikipedia

Age: 27 | Sport: Basketball | Nationality: Greece

Giannis Antetokounmpo is the only player in this year's top ten still in his 20s, with Neymar turning 30 in February. The two-time MVP of the Milwaukee Bucks inked a five-year, $228 million contract in December 2020, which is currently the richest contract in the NBA in terms of overall value. In addition to signing a licensing agreement with NFT platform NFTSTAR and adding WhatsApp and Google's Pixel 6 phone to his roster of endorsements, he was one of the investors in the $165 million investment round for timepiece reselling platform WatchBox that was revealed in November. Additionally, Rise, a biography about Antetokounmpo, will be available to stream on Disney+.
